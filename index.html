Cells and DNA

I'm a fifth-year Molecular Engineering PhD student in the Seelig Lab at the University of Washington. I'm working to engineer synthetic CRISPR-based gene circuits in mammalian cells. My research interests lie in biocomputing and using weird endogenous biological systems to make cool stuff.

I graduated with a BS in Biochemistry and Cell Biology and an MS in Biology from UC San Diego, where I worked in the the Hasty Lab. Here, I built a toolkit of inducible promoters for budding yeast.

Code

I like building web apps, weird tools, and games. I'm very comfortable with Python (Flask) and JavaScript for web development. I have a decent amount of experience with web development and cloud deployment technologies (Docker, AWS, DigitalOcean, MongoDB, nginx, etc), but I never have fun using them. I'm also comfortable with Python, R, and MatLab for data analysis, visualization, and machine learning.

Easy Gene DB is a custom gene database that I built from scratch and manually deployed on because every other gene database had too many features, and it was hard to find the things I really needed (transcripts and CDSs).

Wiki Wall Street is the stock market game for Wikipedia articles, where you play by buying and selling articles based on their page views.

BeTeal is a Python tool to reorganize your BeReal recap from the least teal to most teal frames, although I don't think anyone other than me even uses BeReal anymore...

More code projects on my GitHub!

Plastic and Wires

I make machines. Software-wise I'm proficient with Fusion360 for 3D printing, KiCAD for PCB design, and C++ for Arduino programming.

A machine that mixes skim milk and heavy cream to make milks and creams of varying fat content. It consists of a couple of relay-controlled peristaltic pumps and an Arduino in a 3D printed shell.

A machine to let you reach the top of your phone screen without using both hands. It's just a stepper motor in a weird 3D printed phone case.

A machine that lets you know when your jello is set. I hypothesized that jello's electrical resistance increases as it sets. I was right.

More machines also on my GitHub!
